# UX Brief

## Project

Just Life Clothing is a clean, everyday apparel website focused on women's and men's clothing. The experience should feel modern, warm, easy to shop, and credible enough for a portfolio retail brand.

## Primary Audience

- Shoppers browsing casual clothing for everyday outfits.
- Students or young professionals who want simple style choices without a complex luxury feel.
- Mobile-first visitors who expect quick browsing, clear pricing, and fast add-to-cart actions.

## Business Goals

- Help visitors choose between women's and men's collections immediately.
- Highlight new arrivals, sale items, and best sellers.
- Make product browsing feel organized and trustworthy.
- Keep add-to-cart interactions simple and visible.
- Encourage email signup without interrupting shopping.

## User Goals

- Quickly understand what Just Life sells.
- Browse products by department and category.
- Compare product names, prices, sizes, and visual style.
- Add an item to the cart after choosing a size.
- See cart count and total without leaving the page.

## Experience Principles

- Clear first impression: shoppers should see apparel, brand name, and shopping paths in the first viewport.
- Low friction: primary actions should be obvious and close to product information.
- Editorial but practical: imagery can feel stylish, while navigation and product grids stay easy to scan.
- Consistent retail patterns: familiar category tabs, filter/sort controls, product cards, and cart panel.
- Mobile confidence: no cramped text, no hidden product actions, and readable tap targets.

## Page Priorities

1. Home page: brand impression, department entry points, trend collections, new arrivals, promotion, newsletter.
2. Collection pages: department title, category tabs, filter/sort toolbar, product grid, quick add.
3. Cart panel: item list, size, price, remove action, running total, clear cart.
4. Footer: store links, contact, social links.

## Success Criteria

- A visitor can reach women's or men's products in one click from the home page.
- A visitor can identify product price and size options without opening another page.
- Cart feedback is immediate after adding a product.
- Layout works at desktop, tablet, and phone sizes.
- Visual design feels cohesive across home, collection, and cart surfaces.
